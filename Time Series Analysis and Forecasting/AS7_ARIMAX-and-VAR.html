The fitted line from the plot demonstrates an impeccable linear relationship between log(CPI) and log(Cost), which validates the existence of linearity between them. Based on the model summary, we notice a tiny p-value of less than 2.2e-16, prompting us to discard the null hypothesis and infer that log(Cost) significantly impacts log(CPI). The coefficient for log(CPI) stands at 1.303, indicating that with every percentage point increase in Cost, CPI elevates by 1.303 percentage points.

However, upon examining the ACF plot for residuals, we notice the presence of both seasonality and trend. With multiple significant lags in the residuals and a p-value less than 2.2e-16, we ascertain that the residuals are not a simple white noise. As such, the linear model incorporating time series components isnt an optimal model in this situation.

The auto.arima() function returns a model of Regression with ARIMA(0,1,1)(0,1,2)[12] errors, with an AICc of -863.79 and BIC of -848.38. The Ljung-Box test yields a P-value is above the threshold of 0.05, making us fail to reject the null hypothesis. This suggests that the residuals can be treated as white noise, indicating that they are independently distributed and show serial correlation. Analysis of the residuals from the ACF plot reveals a normal shape but with a heavier tail on the distribution plot. An anomaly is seen at lag 18, making it challenging to confirm if its white noise. Nevertheless, according to the test result, it is considered white noise.
